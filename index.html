How to Write a Letter to Santa Claus
Co-authored by wikiHow Staff
A well written letter shows Santa you are polite. Plus, it makes it easier for him to get you the presents you want. After all, with millions of people asking for presents, you could say he is a pretty busy guy. Start by thinking about what you want to ask for in advance. Then write a polite letter, decorate it, and give it to your parents to send it off to the North Pole.
View Image Larger
#1 Make a list of the things you want a few days before.

Start out by writing down everything you think you might want a couple of days before you write your letter to Santa. Return to your list every so often and reconsider what you wrote. Cross off items you are not keen on, keeping only the ones you really love.[1] Santa gets tons of letters from children (and children-at-heart) around the globe, so sometimes he is not able to get a child everything on their list. This is why it is important that you only include your top items.

View Image Larger
#2 Put on some Christmas tunes

You should be full to the brim with Christmas spirit when you write your letter to Santa and there is nothing like a little Christmas music to get the job done. You can play Christmas music on the radio, your phone, or even on a computer. Ask your parents if you need help.
